Paper Note: Augmenting Fault Samples in Learnable Feature Space (Feature-level SMOTE)

Addressing high overlap and class imbalance in fault diagnosis of gas turbines.

Paper Title: Feature-level SMOTE: Augmenting fault samples in learnable feature space for imbalanced fault diagnosis of gas turbines

Journal: Expert Systems With Applications (Elsevier), Vol. 238, 2024

DOI: 10.1016/j.eswa.2023.122023

1. Background

Training deep learning models for fault diagnosis in gas turbines faces two main challenges:

  1. Class Imbalance: Normal samples far outnumber fault samples in real operations.
  2. Inter-class Overlap: Early fault characteristics are highly similar to normal states in the raw data space.

Conventional methods like SMOTE generate synthetic samples in the raw space, which often creates noise in overlapping regions. This paper proposes Feature-level SMOTE: mapping data to a discriminative feature space before performing augmentation.

Fig 1. Sample distributions in raw vs. learnable feature space.

2. Methodology: Feature-level SMOTE

The framework consists of three steps: Space Mapping, Feature-level Augmentation, and Classification.

2.1 Space Mapping

Fig 2. Architecture of Deep Siamese Multi-head Self-attention Network (DSMHSA).

To solve overlap, DSMHSA is used as the feature extractor:

  • Network: Transformer-based multi-head self-attention for capturing long-range dependencies in time series.
  • Strategy: Siamese structure with contrastive loss to pull same-class samples closer and push different classes apart.

2.2 Feature-level Augmentation

Once a discriminative space is obtained, SMOTE is applied to the latent features:

  • Operation: Performed on latent feature representations instead of raw signals.
  • Advantage: Larger "Safe Regions" in the feature space ensure high-quality synthetic samples without generating noise in overlapping zones.

2.3 Fault Classification

The balanced feature set is then fed into a Softmax Classifier (SMC) for final fault diagnosis.

3. Experimental Validation

Fig 3. Performance data collection and sample construction.

Tested on a real dataset from an Asian airline and a public robot failure dataset:

  • Performance: Achieved a Balanced Accuracy of 90.38% on the gas turbine dataset.
  • Comparison: Outperformed 7 mainstream methods including standard SMOTE and ADASYN.
  • Visualization: t-SNE confirms that the "Separate before Augment" strategy effectively isolates healthy and faulty samples.

4. Conclusion

Feature-level SMOTE highlights the necessity of feature space optimization before data augmentation, providing a robust solution for imbalanced industrial data.
